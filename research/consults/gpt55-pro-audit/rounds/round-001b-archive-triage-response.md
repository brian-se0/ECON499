BATCH_PLAN

Batch 1: Surface construction, grid, timestamp, and masks
Decide the audit standard for the raw-to-surface target: coordinate system, moneyness definition, maturity convention, 15:45 availability, observed-cell mask preservation, completed-grid construction, and how interpolation errors should be reported separately from observed-cell errors.

Batch 2: Static arbitrage and total variance
Decide the exact diagnostics and penalties required: price monotonicity/convexity, butterfly arbitrage, calendar arbitrage, total variance monotonicity across maturities, SVI/eSSVI relevance, and whether the neural model can ever be called “arbitrage-free.” Default expected outcome: call it arbitrage-aware unless hard constraints guarantee no static arbitrage over the evaluated domain.

Batch 3: Forecasting models and benchmarks
Decide minimum academically defensible benchmark set: no-change/random-walk surface, ridge, elastic net, HAR/factor benchmark, functional/factor alternatives, and neural model. Also decide direct vs recursive horizons, grid-level loss aggregation, and whether model comparisons use observed cells, completed cells, or both.

Batch 4: Walk-forward validation and HPO
Decide the serialized split contract: train/validation/test dates, embargo or blocked validation if needed, HPO only inside training/validation windows, no randomized CV, all preprocessing fit inside the training window only, and manifest contents needed to reproduce every split.

Batch 5: Forecast evaluation and statistical testing
Decide the required forecast store schema, alignment checks, loss panels, DM/HAC settings, bootstrap settings, MCS/SPA usage, multiple-testing discipline, and proxy/target reliability caveats when completed-grid values are used.

Batch 6: Hedging evaluation later, deferred
Do not audit hedging findings until the hedging-only papers are read. When activated, decide revaluation timing, hedge instrument availability, transaction costs, use of 15:45 spot, early-close behavior, and whether PnL is attributable to forecast quality rather than leakage or revaluation inconsistency.
